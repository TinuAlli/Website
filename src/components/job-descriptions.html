  • Job Title: Program Lead (12 - 15 Months contract) Maternity Leave Coverage

    Reports To: Executive Director

    Department/Group: Program Lead

    Location: Work location may include your home office and the BBVA office

    Company Description

    Black Business Ventures Association (BBVA) is a 100% black-led non-profit organization with a mandate to discover, educate and mentor black tech entrepreneurs to create social and economic prosperity. Our programs and services are designed and delivered through a relatable learning mechanism that is inclusive of cultural context to help entrepreneurs grow and scale their business. BBVA is launching its new Black Accelerator Program, a 3-month program designed to support black-led, early-stage, tech or technology-enabled businesses. BBVA is on a mission to help establish 1000 successful, Black-led tech and tech-enabled businesses in Canada by 2030. The vision of BBVA is to foster a vibrant Canadian technology ecosystem with significant participation by the Black community.

    Job Description

    RESPONSIBILITIES

    BBVA is recruiting for a full-time or part-time Program Specialist to support the BBVA team through strategic planning around the development of programs that will impact the Black tech entrepreneurship ecosystem. The ideal candidate is passionate about startups, technology, innovation, and Black founders. The main responsibilities are:

    • Architect business foundational and tech-based accelerator programs for various cohorts

    • Cultivate a network of Canadian coaches and North American based advisors

    • Lead and support the overall planning and operations of BBVA’s programs

    • Work alongside the BBVA team including facilitators, coaches, and mentors to ensure a successful program and coaching objectives and delivery is met

    • Connect with cohort founders along their program journey to ensure mentorship, program satisfaction and resource delivery

    • Ensure curriculum meets the highest standards in the tech ecosystem – liaise with facilitators to ensure effective delivery of program curriculum

    • Cultivate BBVA’s additional programs including - student summer programs and women-focused tech programs

    QUALIFICATIONS AND EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS

    The ideal Program Lead will have an adept knowledge of the world of tech entrepreneurship and startups. The candidate will be detail oriented and feel comfortable cultivating processes to lead the program team. What we look for in this profile:

    • Passion toward nurturing and helping Black founders succeed in the tech ecosystem

    • An excellent communicator and forward-facing team member, ready to cultivate a network and build relationships within the ecosystem

    • A solutions-oriented, creative thinker with the ability to strategically plan and execute within a team

    • Excitement to directly work with tech startups in an operational or administrative capacity

    • A give-first attitude toward their community and all the individuals they interact with and a passion for helping startups become successful businesses

    BEHAVIORAL COMPETENCIES

    • Openness to Intercultural Learning- the ability to manage biases and use curiosity to learn more about others while also learning about yourself and remaining open to feedback

    • Self-Awareness - the ability to understand oneself and the impact that one’s perceptions and attitudes have on others

    • Collaborative- the ability to create inclusive spaces that value, build upon, and recognize the diverse strengths and expertise of your colleagues

    • Focus on outcomes and plan/drive for results- maximizing resources to achieve results that are aligned with the goals of the organization, while maintaining accountability to each other and external stakeholders

    • Agility- function as a role model of how to effectively work through change and how to adapt your approach, even in times of uncertainty and ambiguity

    • Lead- positively influence others to support achieving solutions to complex issues
